Computer engineer Ray Tomlinson sent the first electronic mail message from one computer to another on the ARPANET network using the @ symbol. Tomlinson has since forgotten what the message said, possibly "Test123" or "QWERTYUIOP".

The first electronic mail standard was proposed at DARPA, including common aspects such as the to and from fields, along with the ability to forward an email.

The first electronically sent advertisement was sent to 400 people by Gary Thuerk from the Digital Equipment Corp. promoting computers. This promotion resulted in $13 million in sales. This email is also considered the first spam message.

The term email became popular replacing terms like "electronic mail" and "network mail". Also, the first smiley face or emoji was used by Scott Fahlman ":)".

The Simple Mail Transfer Protocol was added to the ARPANET. This made it possible to transfer email between different servers and computer networks. Today many servers still use the SMTP.

CompuServe launched the first email program for users to exchange messages at home or intraoffice.

AOL lead email to become a core feature of computer use by offering a free email address with their internet services. Throughout the 1990's America Online bbecame the largest email providor with around 9 million subscribers.

Internet acronyms like LOL were officially recognized in the Oxford English Dictionary. Also, the FTC created email spam laws in efforts to protect privacy online.

Google made their free Gmail services available to the public around the world.

Email is officially mobile when Steve Jobs introduces the iPhone.

While not many huge changes have occured, there are around 4.48 billion email users world wide. It is estimated that the average user has 2 accounts, around 400 billion emails are sent and recieved each day, and in 2022 48.63% of the emails sent each day were spam.
